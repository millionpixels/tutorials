Getting Started

Music making for movies requires us to make technology our friend and learn to interact with tools to create the sound we want.

Different notes generate different feelings. Combination of these notes and chords can create a sense of attachment of the viewer with the scene. It is, thus, important for us to get acquainted with software and techniques involved in audio processing. Here are some basic terms you need to know before we begin:

  • • Digital Audio Workstation (DAW) – It is the software that hosts all the MIDI and audio files that we work with and enables us to manipulate these as we require.
  • • Virtual Studio Technology (VST) – These are plugins that can be added to the DAWs to achieve any sound effect and create sounds to simulate real instruments.
  • • MIDI – Short for Musical Instrument Digital Interface, it is a technical standard that describes a protocol and allows a wide variety of electronic musical instruments, computers and other related devices to connect and communicate with one another.

Tools required to get started:

  • • Software:
    •    o There are a variety of DAWs available in the market to begin with. However, many of them are very expensive. Whichever DAW you choose to work with, it is important to learn all the features of it so that you can go ahead to bring your creativity into your work.
    •    o We suggest you to go for an easy-to-use DAW like Stagelight to ensure that you spend your time creating music rather than learning how to operate the software. Open Labs’ Stagelight comes with practice tutorials that hardly takes 10 minutes to go through all the features of the software. It can host all VST plugins as well and costs just $9.99. We highly recommend it to you.
    •    o Other DAWs include FL Studio, Pro Tools, Logic Pro, Cubase, Garage Band, Ableton Live etc. Remember, the DAW should be one that enhances your creativity. All of these come with a 30 days trial mode. Try them all if you want and go for the one you feel most comfortable with.
  • • Plugins:
    •    o Generally, DAWs come with a variety of sounds and loop packs. But at times you might need a sound for your scene that your DAW doesn’t have. This is where VST plugins come to rescue.
    •    o Strings: Philharmonik Miroslav by ikmultimedia is a great choice. Cakewalk’s String Section from its Studio Instruments series can be used too.
    •    o Drums & hits: Designer Sound Effects pack comes with realistic loops that are very useful in music scoring.
    •    o There are various other websites that provide free loops for download. We’ll be providing our own soundbank as part of Re-Nova’s Explorer Open Source Project.
  • • Hardware:
    •    o Microphone is the most important hardware that you will need. It is important for music scoring as well as to record dialogues and atmosphere sounds. We suggest you to do a comparison between different types of mic to get the best suited for your situation.
    •    o MIDI Keyboard is an optional tool that can enhance your speed of composition process. It is not mandatory since DAWs have piano roll feature to add notes but it is always good be with an instrument to have better feel.
    •    o Audio interface is another optional tool that you’ll require to connect your mic to the computer in order to record the audio and convert it into digital signal. Make sure you buy a portable one which is small in size. M-Audio provides quality hardware at affordable price.

All these together constitute the components required to start making tracks for your movies. General priority order that works in all budget cases is DAW, VST Plugins, Mic, Audio interface, MIDI keyboard.
